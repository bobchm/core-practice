Awakening - Dilullo

  • What is here that is not a thought or image?
  • What is this experience like if I have nothing to compare it to?
  • What is this like when I don’t reference the past, or the future, or even the present?
  • What is this like when I dissolve all expectations of what it should be like?
  • Physically experience where the sense of you the thinker, the sense of the thoughts on the inner movie screen, and the gap between thoughts are all seamlessly one. Just stay there. It might feel dynamic, and it might feel very still.

Joy is exactly what's happening, minus our opinion of it. -- Charlotte Joko Beck

Our practice is a refinement of joy. By joy I don’t mean happiness. Joy has more to do with curiosity. We need to regain the capacity to feel curiosity about everything in our life, even the disasters. Through our efforts at self-protection, we’ve lost most of our curiosity about life. When we’re depressed, we just want to make the depression stop. Likewise when we’re worried, lonely, or confused. Instead, we need to approach our state of mind with curiosity and open wonder. That open, curious listening to life is joy—no matter what the mood of our life is. A person who is totally curious is neither happy nor unhappy. - Charlotte Joko Beck

As you go through your day, through your little upsets and difficulties, ask yourself, “Where is the wonder?” It’s always there. Wonder is the nature of life itself. If we don’t feel it, we just keep doing our practice; we can’t force ourselves to feel it. -- Charlotte Joko Beck

It simply means to not believe in our thoughts. When we don't believe in our thoughts we are always awakened. When we believe in our thoughts we are unawakened. -- Anam Thubten

  2. The suppression of hindrances that have not yet arisen

  4. The maintenance of wholesome mental factors that have already arisen
