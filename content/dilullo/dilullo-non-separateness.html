Non-Separateness

Non-separation, seeing through the veil of separation of duality, is simpler than people think. If you look closely enough, it's something you're actually adding to experience.

Start by relaxing. Put some attention into consciousness, the mind, or thought. Notice what is there in a non-judgmental way. Let it start to settle. Let your attention dilate out. Sit this way for a few minutes.

Move your attention out into the visual field. See whatever is in front of your face a maybe a little to the periphery. You may perceive objects or maybe just forms, shapes, and colors. Rest your attention there and just take it in.

Start to move your attention slightly toward what feels like you but not moving it from the visual experience like you're following it back to see where it comes in contact with you or something separate from that visual experience that's "here" more than "there".

It may appear at some point like the visual experience "out there" is "in here" or continuous from what would have been perceived as a center or self. All appear as one sort of continuum, but don't overlay that expectation onto your experience.

The key is to keep your attention in the visual flow. See if near and far are information that is in the visual flow itself or if these are something that is added afterward when attention bounces back into the mind. See if self and other are to be found in the visual experience or added on afterward. Is there a line between self and other, subject and object in the actual experience? Is there an "out there" and "in here" without overlaying thoughts on the experience?
